SECTION 1
System Architecture Overview

The Anjiana system is split into two separate web applications that share a single Firebase / Firestore backend database. Any change in the Admin Panel is immediately visible on the storefront — there is no delay or sync step.

ApplicationPurposeKey Files
Storefront (CLothingStore/)Customer-facing shopping siteindex.html, products.html, product-details.html, cart.html, wishlist.html
Admin Panel (admin_anjiana/)Back-office management dashboarddashboard.html, js/admin-dashboard.js

Shared Firestore Collections

CollectionUsed ByPurpose
productsBothProduct catalog displayed on the store
categoriesBothCategory filter options on products page
settings/store_infoBothStore name, currency, shipping fees, social links
bannersStorefrontHomepage hero slides and seasonal grids
couponsStorefront checkoutDiscount codes applied at cart
ordersAdmin + Storefront checkoutCustomer order records
reviewsBothCustomer ratings and admin replies
staffAdmin onlyLogin credentials and role access control
stock_historyAdmin onlyRestock and adjustment audit logs

SECTION 3
Products Management

This is the primary catalog management view. It lists every product from the products Firestore collection and allows administrators to edit or delete them.

How Changes Flow to the Storefront

Admin edits a product → saved to Firestore products
→ Storefront products-logic.js calls getDocs(collection(db, "products")) on next page load
→ Updated products immediately appear on products.html, index.html, wishlist.html

Storefront Pages Affected

  • index.html — Shows 3 featured products on the homepage grid
  • products.html — Full listing with filtering and search
  • product-details.html — Individual product loaded by Firestore document ID via URL ?id=
  • wishlist.html — Saved products fetched from Firestore by ID stored in localStorage

Product Fields & Their Storefront Display

FieldShown Where on Storefront
nameProduct card title, detail page heading, browser tab
priceAll product cards and detail page price display
discount (%)Strike-through original price + sale badge on cards and detail page
imageUrlProduct card thumbnails and detail hero image
categoryCategory tag below product name; used as URL filter keyword
descriptionProduct detail page body text
sizes / sizeStockSize selector bubbles — out-of-stock sizes shown as disabled
colors / sizeColorStockColor swatch buttons; cross-variant real-time stock validation
stockIn Stock / Out of Stock label; disables Add-to-Cart button when 0

Storefront Search & Filtering via URL Parameters

  • ?category=women — Filters products where category field contains "women"
  • ?tag=jackets — Filters by secondary tag keyword
  • ?q=cotton — Text search across product name, category, and description

Variant Stock System (Size x Color Matrix)

When both sizes and colors are selected, a stock matrix is generated creating a sizeColorStock map in Firestore (e.g. {"M_Red": 5, "L_Blue": 12}). The storefront uses this map to:

  • Grey-out unavailable size/color combinations in real-time as the customer selects options
  • Enforce exact stock limits when adding to cart (prevents over-ordering)
  • Show "Out of Stock" and disable Add-to-Cart when no stock remains for the selected combination

SECTION 5
Category Management

Categories define the organizational structure of the product catalog. They are stored in Firestore categories collection and control how products are grouped and filtered on the storefront.

Category Data Structure

FieldDescriptionExample
nameCategory display nameJackets
genderDepartment associationMen, Women, Kids, Unisex
parentOptional parent (creates sub-category)Men
imageThumbnail URL shown in category gridshttps://...

How Categories Flow to the Storefront

  1. Admin creates a category (e.g. "Jackets" under parent "Men") — saved to Firestore categories.
  2. When adding a product, the admin form's category dropdown dynamically loads from this collection, grouped by department.
  3. Selected category is saved as the product's category field (e.g. "Men • Jackets").
  4. On the storefront, customers click a navigation link (e.g. "Men") which triggers products.html?category=men.
  5. The storefront's loadProducts() filters products where data.category contains that keyword.
💡 Tip: Category names use a contains-text match. A product with category "Men • Jackets" will appear when filtering by ?category=men OR ?category=jackets.

SECTION 6
Inventory Management

The Inventory view provides a unified table of all product variant stock levels, restock actions, and a timestamped history log.

Stock Level Indicators

StatusThresholdAdmin DisplayStorefront Effect
In StockStock > 5Normal textAdd-to-Cart enabled
Low StockStock 1–5Red text + badgeAdd-to-Cart still works; alert shown to admin
Out of StockStock = 0Highlighted in redAdd-to-Cart disabled; "Out of Stock" label shown

Restock Process

  1. Admin clicks Restock next to a variant.
  2. A modal shows current stock and a quantity input.
  3. Admin enters units to add and submits.
  4. New stock total is written back to Firestore products document.
  5. A record is logged to stock_history with timestamp and operator name.
  6. Storefront immediately reflects the updated stock — the product becomes purchasable again.

The Stock History Log panel displays the last 10 adjustments with product, variant, quantity delta, timestamp, and who made the change.

SECTION 7
Payment Ledger

The Payments view is a complete financial transaction log built by reading all order records from the orders collection.

Summary KPI Cards

MetricCalculation
Gross Sales (Paid)Sum of totalAmount for orders where paymentStatus = "Paid"
Refunds ProcessedSum of totalAmount for orders where paymentStatus = "Refunded"
Net RevenueGross Sales minus Refunds total

Refund Action

For any "Paid" order, clicking Refund updates the order's paymentStatus to "Refunded" in Firestore. The KPI cards and ledger reflect this change immediately on next load.

💡 Note: Payment data originates from the storefront's checkout. The Admin Payment Ledger only reads and manages existing transaction data — it does not create payments itself.

SECTION 9
Discounts & Banners

This section has two independent panels: Coupon Code creator and Homepage Banner/Campaign manager.

Coupon Codes

Coupons are stored in the Firestore coupons collection. The storefront checkout page reads and validates these codes when a customer applies one.

Coupon TypeHow It Works at Checkout
Percentage (%)Reduces cart total by the set percentage (e.g. 20% off)
Fixed Amount (LKR)Deducts a flat amount from cart total (e.g. Rs. 500 off)
Buy 2 Get 1 Free (B2G1)Applies buy-two-get-one logic at checkout

Each coupon also has: a Minimum Purchase Amount (cart must exceed this), an Expiry Date, and an Active/Inactive Status switch.

Homepage Banners & Campaigns

Banners are stored in the Firestore banners collection and control the visual content of the homepage promotional sections.

Banner TypeStorefront Location
Top Hero BannerFull-width hero slider at the top of index.html — auto-cycles every 6 seconds
Seasonal Collection Grid2–3 column image grid section below the hero
Featured Collection SpotlightFeatured product/collection section further down the homepage
  1. Admin creates a banner with title, image URL, link, and section type — saved to Firestore banners.
  2. On page load, index.html queries banners filtered by type and renders them into the correct homepage section.
  3. Multiple banners of the same type are displayed together in that section (slider or grid).

SECTION 10
Reviews & Feedback

The Reviews view manages all customer-submitted product ratings stored in the Firestore reviews collection.

Review Workflow

  1. Customer submits a star rating and text review on the storefront's product-details.html page.
  2. Review is saved to Firestore reviews with product ID, customer email, rating (1–5), comment, and timestamp.
  3. Review appears in the Admin's moderation table with status "Pending".
  4. Admin can Approve the review (makes it publicly visible on the storefront) or Reject it (permanently hides it).
  5. Admin can write an Admin Reply that appears below the customer's review on the storefront product page.
💡 Storefront Display: Only reviews with status = "Approved" are fetched and shown on product detail pages. The average star rating is calculated dynamically from all approved reviews for that product.
⚠️ Note: Reviews are linked to products by productId. If a product is deleted, its reviews remain in the database but won't display since the product page no longer exists.

SECTION 11
Reports & Analytics

The Reports view provides visual and numerical business intelligence. It does not directly affect the storefront but gives management data to make informed decisions about the catalog and pricing.

Analytics Scope Selector

Switch between Daily, Weekly, and Monthly granularity. The sales chart updates accordingly to show trends at the selected time resolution.

Key Metrics Calculated

MetricHow It Is Calculated
Total Stock ValuationSum of (stock quantity × price) for every product and variant
Return Rate(Returned orders ÷ Total orders) × 100%
Exchange Rate(Exchange requests ÷ Total orders) × 100%
Customer Growth (30 days)Count of customer accounts created in the past 30 days

Top Viewed Products

Displays products ranked by view count — tracked when customers open product detail pages on the storefront. Helps identify high-interest items that may not be converting to sales.

Detailed Sales Chart

A Chart.js bar/line chart built by grouping all order totalAmount values by the selected date granularity. Gives a visual revenue trend over time.

SECTION 12
Staff Management

Controls who has access to the Admin Panel and what they can do. Staff accounts are stored in the Firestore staff collection.

Role Permission Levels

RoleAccess LevelRestricted From
StaffBasic — view products and process basic tasks onlyCategories, Payments, Shipping, Discounts, Staff, Settings
ManagerExtended — edit catalog, process returns and refundsStaff management and system-level settings
AdministratorFull root access — unrestricted to all sectionsNothing

Approval Status

  • Approved — Staff can log in and access the admin panel
  • Pending — Account awaiting approval; login is blocked
  • Denied — Login access permanently blocked

Login History Tracker

Records each admin login with staff email, browser/device info, and timestamp — creating a full audit trail of who accessed the admin panel and when.

💡 Storefront Impact: Staff accounts only control Admin Panel access. Customer-facing shoppers are entirely separate and are not managed here.

Currency Setting — Full Impact

When the Currency Symbol is changed and saved, the following update across the entire system:

  • Dashboard KPI cards (Total Sales display)
  • Products list price columns
  • Order management table amounts
  • Payment ledger transaction amounts
  • All printed invoice receipts (subtotal, shipping, grand total)
  • Analytics stock valuation figures
  • Storefront product prices (if currency is read from Firestore by the storefront)

Available options: LKR (Rs.), USD ($), EUR (€), GBP (£), JPY (¥), INR (Rs)
